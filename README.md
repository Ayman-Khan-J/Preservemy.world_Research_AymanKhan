Semester 1

PROJECT 1: Modularity
Inspired by the spinal cord, this project involved studying an existing modular system, abstracting its logic, and developing a new system governed by defined growth rules. The final outcome consisted of 100 laser-cut German board modules assembled through dry joinery without adhesives. The resulting structure replicated the flexibility and movement characteristics of a biological spine.

PROJECT 2:Parasitic Growth
Modularity II
An extension of the Vertebral System, this project imagined the modular system as a parasitic organism occupying existing sites. The system selectively attached itself to metallic elements of structures while avoiding plastic components. Constructed from 200 laser-cut modules, the installation explored growth, adaptation, and occupation.
Displayed at SADA Open House.
Semester 2

PROJECT 3:Hybrid Object
Dadaism & Surrealism
A study of object transformation through the lens of Dadaist and Surrealist thinking. By combining a plastic water bottle and a pipe, the project explored opposing material characteristics and merged them into a single hybrid artifact that challenged conventional object identities.

Project 4: Seating Artifact
The Oblique
Designed and fabricated collaboratively within a team of six students. The project investigated posture, balance, and discomfort through a seating artifact that accommodated a specific body position on a 60-degree incline. Inspired by natural slopes, the artifact explored the sensation of instability and bodily awareness.
Displayed at SADA Open House.

PROJECT 5:In Conversation
Spatial Intervention
A site-specific proposal investigating blocked emergency exits on campus. Drawing from the ideas of the Situationist International, the project examined the conflict between the primary and secondary functions of space. The intervention aimed to reveal hidden spatial conditions and provoke discussion around safety, occupation, and user behavior.
Displayed at SADA Open House.
