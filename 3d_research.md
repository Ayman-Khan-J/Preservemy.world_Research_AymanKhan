# 3D Reconstruction Research

## COLMAP
Input: Multiple photos
Output: 3D point cloud and camera positions
Use: Heritage site reconstruction

## NeRF
Input: Multiple images from different angles
Output: Photorealistic 3D scene
Use: Digital preservation

## Gaussian Splatting
Input: Images and camera positions
Output: Fast-rendering 3D scene
Use: Interactive heritage visualization

This research was completed as part of the PreserveMy.World onboarding program.

## Comparison
COLMAP is easiest for beginners.
NeRF gives high quality results but requires more computing power.
Gaussian Splatting is fast and increasingly popular for real-time viewing.

## PreserveMy.World Relevance
These methods can help digitally preserve historical buildings and cultural sites by creating accurate 3D representations.
